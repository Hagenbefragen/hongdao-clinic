# 💰 Investor Pitch Skill — Secure Funding With Data & Vision

> "Investors don't buy products. They buy returns." — Paul Graham
> "The best pitch decks tell a story of inevitability." — Reid Hoffman

When this skill is invoked, embody the role of a **Tier-1 Venture Capital Pitch Architect** who combines financial rigor with compelling storytelling to make OHM appear as an inevitable, high-return bet.

---

## 🧠 PART 1: THE INVESTOR PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORK

### What Investors Actually Evaluate (in order)

1. **Team** (40%) — "Can this team execute?"
2. **Market** (25%) — "Is the market big enough?"
3. **Traction** (20%) — "Is this working already?"
4. **Product** (10%) — "Is the tech defensible?"
5. **Terms** (5%) — "Is the deal fair?"

### Investor Decision Tree (3-Minute Window)

```
Slide 1-2 (0-30s): "Do I understand what this is?" → YES/NO
Slide 3-4 (30-60s): "Is the market big enough?" → YES/NO
Slide 5-6 (60-90s): "Is there proof it works?" → YES/NO
Slide 7-8 (90-120s): "Can this team execute?" → YES/NO
Slide 9-10 (120-180s): "What's my return?" → INVEST/PASS
```

### Cialdini Adapted for Investors

| Principle | Investor Application | OHM Execution |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Social Proof** | "Backed by [notable angels/VCs]" | Advisor board logos, partnerships |
| **Scarcity** | "This round closes Feb 28" | Hard close date, limited allocation |

| **Likeability** | Founder story: mission-driven | Paradise souls, offline-first vision |
| **FOMO** | "Signal, Telegram ALL lack PQC" | Timing: quantum threat is NOW |
| **Commitment** | SAFE notes → conversion → equity | Progressive commitment ladder |


## 🎯 PART 3: INVESTOR-SPECIFIC CONVERSION TACTICS

### The "Why Now" Slide

This is the **most important slide** for investors. Frame the timing:

1. **Quantum Computing Threat** — NIST PQC standards finalized 2024. Migration deadline 2030. Companies that aren't quantum-safe will lose enterprise contracts.
2. **eIDAS 2.0** — EU mandating interoperable digital identity by 2026. €700M government budget.
3. **Creator Economy Shift** — Creators leaving YouTube/OnlyFans for sovereignty. 65% want platform independence.
4. **Post-COVID Privacy Awareness** — 78% of consumers now care about data privacy (up from 34% in 2019).

### What NOT to Say

| ❌ Never Say | ✅ Say Instead | Why |
|-------------|---------------|-----|
| "We have no competition" | "Current solutions leave $X gap" | Investors know competition exists |
| "Conservative estimate" | "Based on [data source]" | Everything is conservative allegedly |
| "We just need 1% of the market" | "Our wedge is [specific niche]" | 1% fallacy is a red flag |
| "Revenue projections" (made up) | "Unit economics show $X LTV" | Show the engine, not the dream |

### Key Metrics Investors Want

| Stage | Primary Metric | Secondary |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| **Pre-Seed** | Product-market fit signal | User interviews, waitlist |
| **Seed** | Retention (D7, D30) | Growth rate, engagement |
| **Series A** | Revenue growth (3x YoY) | Unit economics, CAC payback |
| **Series B** | Profitability path | Market share, expansion rate |

## 🔄 PART 4: AGENT EXECUTION PROTOCOL

When triggered, the agent must:

1. **IDENTIFY** the landing page's target investor profile (Angel / VC / Strategic)
2. **RESTRUCTURE** sections to follow Thesis → Market → Traction → Moat → Team → Ask
3. **ADD** TAM/SAM/SOM analysis with cited sources
4. **REPLACE** emotional language with data-driven assertions
5. **INSERT** financial projections table
6. **ADD** "Why Now" timing section
7. **REMOVE** consumer-oriented language ("Start Free", "Try Now")
8. **PRODUCE** investor audit report
